### How Users Read on the Web

**Key finding (1997, validated repeatedly):** Users don't read web pages — they scan. 79% of users scan any new page; only 16% read word-by-word.

**Implications for content design:**
- Use highlighted keywords (bold, links, color)
- Use meaningful sub-headings (not "clever" ones)
- Use bulleted lists
- One idea per paragraph
- Use the inverted pyramid: start with the conclusion
- Use half the word count (or less) of conventional writing

### The F-Pattern (2006, updated 2020)

Eye-tracking studies show users scan in an F-shaped pattern. The first two lines get the most attention, then users scan vertically down the left side.

**Content design takeaways:**
- The first 11 characters (~2 words) of any heading are critical
- Front-load keywords in headings, links, and bullet points
- Don't start lines with the same word (causes "skipping" behavior)
- Important info should never live only in the middle or end of a paragraph

### Error Message Research

NNG's error message guidelines based on decades of usability testing:

1. **Be visible** — Errors must appear near the problem, not just at the top of the page
2. **Use plain language** — "That email address isn't valid" not "Validation error: field_email regex mismatch"
3. **Be specific** — "Enter a date after today" not "Invalid date"
4. **Be constructive** — Always suggest how to fix the problem
5. **Don't blame the user** — "We couldn't find that page" not "You typed a wrong URL"
6. **Use polite, non-alarming tone** — Avoid red exclamation marks for minor issues

### Form Design Research

- **Labels above fields** outperform left-aligned labels (faster completion, fewer errors)
- **Placeholder text as labels** causes usability problems (disappears on focus, low contrast)
- **Inline validation** (real-time feedback) reduces errors by 22% compared to post-submit validation
- **"Optional" labels** are better than asterisks for required fields — mark the minority
- **Descriptive submit buttons** ("Create account") outperform generic ones ("Submit")

### Notification Research

- Users ignore notifications they don't understand within 3 seconds
- **Action-oriented notifications** (with clear CTA) get 3x more engagement
- **Grouped notifications** reduce notification fatigue
- **Previewing consequences** ("Mute for 1 hour") increases user confidence

### Mobile Content Research

- **Mobile users are not "on the go"** — 68% of mobile usage is at home
- **Mobile users want the same content** as desktop users, not a stripped-down version
- **Thumb zones** affect content placement: primary actions in easy-reach areas
- **Touch target size:** Minimum 48×48px for buttons with text labels
- **Mobile reading:** Even more scanning-heavy than desktop — every word must justify its existence

### Microcopy Research

- **Button labels:** Specific verbs outperform generic ones ("Add to cart" > "Submit")
- **Confirmation dialogs:** The question in the title should match the action buttons ("Delete this file?" → "Delete" / "Keep")
- **Empty states:** Users who see helpful empty states are 40% more likely to complete the suggested action
- **Tooltips:** Effective for progressive disclosure but must be triggered on hover AND focus (accessibility)

### Trust and Credibility

- **Spelling and grammar errors** are the #1 factor that reduces credibility
- **Clear pricing information** increases trust more than testimonials
- **Explaining "why"** when asking for personal information reduces abandonment
- **Consistent terminology** between marketing and product builds trust
