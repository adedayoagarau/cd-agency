### Core Principle

Plain language means your audience can find what they need, understand what they find, and act on that understanding — the first time they read it.

### The Five Rules of Plain Language

1. **Write for your reader, not yourself.** Identify your audience before writing. A developer reading API docs has different needs than a first-time user seeing an onboarding screen.

2. **Use simple, common words.** Replace jargon with everyday language. "Use" not "utilize." "Start" not "initiate." "End" not "terminate." If a 12-year-old wouldn't know the word, reconsider it.

3. **Use short sentences.** Aim for 15-20 words per sentence. One idea per sentence. Break complex instructions into numbered steps.

4. **Use active voice.** "You can save your work" not "Your work can be saved." Active voice is shorter, clearer, and assigns responsibility.

5. **Front-load the important information.** Put the action or key message first. "Save your changes before closing" not "Before closing, make sure to save your changes."

### Plain Language Patterns for UI

| Instead of | Write |
|-----------|-------|
| "An error has occurred" | "Something went wrong" |
| "Authentication failed" | "Wrong email or password" |
| "Insufficient permissions" | "You don't have access" |
| "Operation completed successfully" | "Done" or specific outcome |
| "Please be advised that" | (delete entirely) |
| "In order to" | "To" |
| "At this point in time" | "Now" |
| "Due to the fact that" | "Because" |
| "It is recommended that" | "We recommend" or just state it |

### Reading Level Targets

- **Consumer apps:** Grade 6-8 (age 11-13)
- **Business tools:** Grade 8-10 (age 13-15)
- **Technical docs:** Grade 10-12 (age 15-17)
- **Legal/compliance:** Simplify to Grade 8 where possible

### The "So What?" Test

After writing any piece of content, ask: "So what? What does this mean for the user? What should they do?" If the content doesn't answer these questions, rewrite it.

### Evidence

- The US federal government's plain language initiative reduced call center volume by 50% for some agencies.
- GOV.UK found that content written at a lower reading level was understood by *everyone* — including highly educated readers — faster and with fewer errors.
- Studies show users spend an average of 10-20 seconds on a page before deciding to stay or leave. Plain language increases the chance they stay.

### Application to Content Design

When reviewing or writing any UI text:
1. Read it aloud. If you stumble, simplify.
2. Cut every word that doesn't add meaning.
3. Replace abstract nouns with concrete verbs.
4. Test with users who represent your least technical audience.
5. Prefer the word your user would use to search for this feature.
