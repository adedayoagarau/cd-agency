### Core Principle

**Voice** is your brand's personality — it stays constant. **Tone** is how that personality adapts to different situations — it changes based on context, emotion, and stakes. A doctor has one voice (knowledgeable, caring) but different tones (gentle when delivering bad news, celebratory when sharing good results).

### The Voice/Tone Distinction

| | Voice (constant) | Tone (variable) |
|--|-----------------|-----------------|
| **What** | Brand personality traits | Emotional register per context |
| **Example** | "We're confident and helpful" | Celebratory on success, empathetic on error |
| **Changes?** | No — evolves slowly over years | Yes — shifts per screen/scenario |
| **Defined by** | Brand values, company culture | User emotion, task context, stakes |

### Defining Voice: The 4-Dimension Model

Rate your brand on each spectrum:

1. **Funny ←→ Serious**
   - Funny: Casual humor, playful metaphors, wordplay
   - Serious: Straightforward, no jokes, business-focused

2. **Formal ←→ Casual**
   - Formal: Complete sentences, no contractions, professional
   - Casual: Contractions, fragments, conversational

3. **Respectful ←→ Irreverent**
   - Respectful: Polite, acknowledges user effort, careful
   - Irreverent: Breaks rules, challenges conventions, edgy

4. **Enthusiastic ←→ Matter-of-fact**
   - Enthusiastic: Exclamation marks, superlatives, energy
   - Matter-of-fact: Neutral, lets results speak, understated

### The Tone Spectrum Across UI Contexts

From highest to lowest emotional register:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Celebration** | Enthusiastic, warm | "You did it! Your first project is live." |
| **Onboarding** | Welcoming, encouraging | "Let's get you set up. This takes about 2 minutes." |
| **Neutral UI** | Clear, professional | "Select a template to get started." |
| **Empty states** | Helpful, optimistic | "No messages yet. They'll show up here." |
| **Warnings** | Direct, calm | "This will remove all team members. Are you sure?" |
| **Errors** | Empathetic, solution-focused | "We couldn't save your changes. Check your connection and try again." |
| **Data loss** | Serious, precise | "Deleting your account removes all data permanently. This cannot be undone." |

### Mailchimp's Voice Framework (Industry Standard)

Mailchimp pioneered the public voice and tone guide. Their approach:
- Voice is defined by traits: "Fun but not silly, confident but not cocky, smart but not stodgy, informal but not sloppy, helpful but not overbearing"
- Each trait has a "this, not that" clarification
- Tone adapts based on the user's likely emotional state
- Content types are mapped to appropriate tone levels

### Measuring Voice Consistency

**Voice audit checklist:**
1. Remove the brand name from 5 screens of content. Can you still identify the brand?
2. Read content aloud. Does it sound like the same person wrote all of it?
3. Do error messages match the tone of success messages (adjusted for context)?
4. Would a new writer know how to write for this brand from the guide alone?
5. Are there words the brand always/never uses? (e.g., Slack never says "user" — they say "people")

### The "This, Not That" Pattern

The most effective voice guides define each trait with boundaries:

- **Confident, not arrogant:** "Here's how to fix it" not "Obviously, you should have..."
- **Friendly, not flippant:** "We hit a snag" not "Oopsie daisy!"
- **Direct, not blunt:** "Delete your account" not "Want to abandon everything?"
- **Casual, not sloppy:** Contractions yes, txtspk no
- **Helpful, not condescending:** "Need help?" not "Don't worry, lots of people find this confusing"

### Voice Across Channels

The same voice adapts to channel constraints:
- **Push notification:** Maximum personality density — every word carries brand voice
- **Error message:** Voice takes a back seat to clarity — be helpful first, on-brand second
- **Marketing page:** Voice at full volume — personality, energy, persuasion
- **Legal/compliance:** Voice at minimum — accuracy and clarity override personality
- **Documentation:** Helpful-voice forward — personality is in the examples and asides
