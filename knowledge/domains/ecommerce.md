### Domain Context

E-commerce content has the most direct, measurable impact on revenue of any content domain. Every word on a product page, checkout flow, or confirmation email influences conversion. The discipline here is highly data-driven, with A/B testing as standard practice.

### Key Challenges

1. **Conversion pressure.** Every point of friction in checkout costs revenue.
2. **Trust at a distance.** Users can't touch products — content must bridge the sensory gap.
3. **Returns and complaints.** Unclear product descriptions lead to returns; unclear policies lead to complaints.
4. **Scale.** Large catalogs need content systems, not individual copywriting.

### Content Patterns by Feature

**Product pages:**
- Title: specific, searchable — "Women's Wool Blend Overcoat — Navy, Size M"
- Description: lead with benefits, follow with specs — "Keeps you warm in temperatures down to -10°C. 80% wool, 20% polyester blend."
- Key details upfront: price, availability, shipping, returns — before the fold
- Social proof: reviews, ratings, "bestseller" badges
- Size/fit guidance: "Runs large. We recommend ordering one size down."

**Cart and checkout:**
- Persistent cart summary: items, quantities, prices — always visible
- Shipping costs shown early: don't surprise at checkout (the #1 cause of cart abandonment)
- Trust signals at payment: lock icon, "Secure checkout," accepted payment methods
- Express checkout: "Buy now" or "Checkout with Apple Pay" — minimize steps
- Guest checkout: "Continue as guest" — don't force account creation

**Order confirmation:**
- Specific confirmation: "Order #12345 confirmed. 3 items shipping to 123 Main St."
- Timeline: "Estimated delivery: March 18-20"
- Next steps: "You'll receive a shipping notification with tracking info"
- Easy access: "View your order" button

**Shipping and delivery:**
- Status: "Shipped — In transit — Out for delivery — Delivered" (clear progression)
- Proactive updates: "Your package is arriving today by 8 PM"
- Delays: "Your delivery is delayed. New estimated date: March 22. We're sorry for the inconvenience."
- Failed delivery: "We tried to deliver your package. It will be at your local post office for pickup."

### Conversion Copy Patterns

**Urgency (use ethically):**
- Real urgency: "Sale ends Sunday" / "Only 3 left in stock" (only when true)
- Never fake scarcity or manufactured countdown timers

**Social proof:**
- Reviews: "4.8 stars from 1,247 reviews"
- Specific testimonials: "Fits perfectly, runs true to size" — specificity > generality
- Purchase indicators: "123 people bought this today" (if true and not manipulative)

**Friction reduction:**
- "Free shipping on orders over $50"
- "Free returns within 30 days"
- "No credit card required" (for trials)
- "Cancel anytime" (for subscriptions)

**Upsell/cross-sell:**
- "Frequently bought together" (relevant, not aggressive)
- "Complete the look" (fashion)
- "Customers also viewed" (discovery)
- Never: "Don't forget to add!" or manipulative urgency

### Returns and Support Content

- **Return policy:** Clear, findable, and human — "Changed your mind? Return any unworn item within 30 days for a full refund."
- **Return flow:** "Select items to return → Choose a reason → Print your label → Drop off your package"
- **Refund timeline:** "Refunds typically appear in 5-7 business days after we receive your return."
- **Support:** "Need help? Chat with us" — not hidden behind 5 clicks

### Case Studies

**Amazon:** One-click purchasing, "Buy now" — the ultimate friction reduction. Lesson: every click you remove from checkout is measurable revenue.

**Shopify stores:** Consistent checkout experience across millions of stores. Lesson: standardized, tested checkout copy outperforms creative checkout copy.

**Warby Parker:** "Home Try-On" — content that reframes a limitation (can't try glasses online) as a feature. Lesson: content can turn weaknesses into differentiators.

**Glossier:** Community-driven product descriptions written in customer language, not marketing speak. "Feels like nothing on your skin" > "Our lightweight formula..." Lesson: user language > brand language for product descriptions.
