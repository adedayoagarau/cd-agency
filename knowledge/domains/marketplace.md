### Domain Context

Marketplace content serves two distinct audiences simultaneously — buyers and sellers (or riders and drivers, hosts and guests, freelancers and clients). Content must build trust between strangers, facilitate transactions, and handle disputes. The platform is the middleman, and content IS the trust infrastructure.

### Key Challenges

1. **Two-sided language.** Every feature serves both sides. Content must adapt per perspective.
2. **Trust between strangers.** The platform's content is the guarantee. Profiles, reviews, and policies are trust mechanisms.
3. **Dynamic pricing.** Surge pricing, demand-based fees, variable service costs — content must explain changing prices.
4. **Dispute resolution.** When things go wrong between two people, platform content mediates.

### Content for Each Side

**Buyer/Customer perspective:**
- "Find a plumber near you" (their task, their language)
- "4.9 stars · 127 jobs completed · Background checked"
- "Your payment is protected until the job is complete"
- "If you're not satisfied, request a refund within 48 hours"

**Seller/Provider perspective:**
- "Get more customers in your area" (their motivation)
- "You've earned $1,234 this week · 8 jobs completed"
- "New request: Kitchen faucet repair · $80-120 · 2 miles away"
- "Respond within 1 hour to improve your ranking"

### Trust-Building Content Patterns

**Profiles:**
```
[Photo] Alex M. · Plumber
★ 4.9 (127 reviews) · 3 years on platform
"Licensed and insured. Specializing in residential plumbing."
Response time: Usually within 1 hour
Completed jobs: 127 · Repeat clients: 34
```

**Verification signals:**
- "Identity verified" / "Background check passed"
- "Licensed professional" / "Certified by [authority]"
- "Superhost" / "Top Rated" / "Pro" badges — earned status
- "Member since 2019" — longevity signals reliability

**Reviews:**
- Prompt both sides to review: "How was your experience with Alex?"
- Structured ratings: Overall + specific dimensions (punctuality, quality, communication)
- Verified reviews only: "This review is from a confirmed transaction"
- Show both positive and negative — authenticity builds more trust than perfection

### Transaction Flow Content

**Matching/discovery:**
```
"3 plumbers available near you"
"Starting at $75/hour · Most arrive within 2 hours"
[Compare] [Book now] [Message first]
```

**Booking/agreement:**
```
"You're booking Alex M. for kitchen faucet repair"
"Estimated: $80-120 · Duration: 1-2 hours"
"Your card will be charged after the job is complete."
[Confirm booking]
```

**In-progress:**
```
"Alex is on the way — arriving in 15 minutes"
"Alex has started the job"
"Alex marked the job as complete. Review the work and approve payment."
```

**Completion:**
```
"Job complete! You paid $95 to Alex M."
"How was your experience?" [★★★★★]
"Tip Alex?" [$5] [$10] [$15] [Custom] [No thanks]
```

### Pricing Content

- **Range estimates:** "$80-120" > "Starting at $80" (sets realistic expectations)
- **Fee transparency:** "Service fee: $8 (10%)" — show the math
- **Dynamic pricing:** "Prices are higher right now due to high demand. Estimated: $24 (usually $15)"
- **Tipping guidance:** "Tips are optional and go directly to your provider" (context, not guilt)
- **Platform take rate:** If relevant to sellers: "You earn 80% of the booking amount"

### Dispute and Support Content

- **Report an issue:** "Something wrong with your order? We're here to help."
- **Dispute flow:** "Tell us what happened" → "We'll review within 24 hours" → "Resolution: [refund/credit/action]"
- **Policy violations:** "Your account has been paused while we review a report. You can respond here."
- **Safety:** "If you feel unsafe, call 911. You can also use our emergency button."

### Case Studies

**Uber:** Real-time status updates. "Your driver is 3 minutes away" with map. Content tracks the physical world in real-time. Lesson: in logistics, content = GPS + context.

**Etsy:** Seller-friendly language. "Your shop" / "Your listings" — ownership language builds seller investment. Buyer-side: "Handmade by Sarah" personalizes the transaction. Lesson: marketplace voice should empower both sides.

**Fiverr:** Service packaging. "I will design your logo for $50" — the seller defines the offer in first person, buyer sees it in a standard format. Lesson: structured content formats standardize quality while preserving personality.

**TaskRabbit:** Task-oriented matching. "I need help with: Moving · Cleaning · Handyman · ..." — starts with the buyer's need, not the provider's skills. Lesson: always start with the demand side's language.
