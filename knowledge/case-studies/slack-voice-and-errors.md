### Overview

Slack is widely cited as one of the best examples of product content design. Their writing is distinctive, human, and functionally excellent. They prove that personality and clarity aren't opposing forces.

### Slack's Voice Principles

1. **Clear over clever.** If a joke or pun makes the message harder to understand, cut it. Clarity always wins.
2. **Human, not robotic.** Write like a helpful, knowledgeable coworker — not a manual or a marketing brochure.
3. **Brevity is a virtue.** Say it in fewer words. Then cut more words.
4. **Never say "user."** Slack talks about "people," "you," "someone," and "your team."

### Error Messages

Slack's error messages are a masterclass in content design:

**Connection errors:**
- "Trouble connecting to Slack. Check your internet and we'll keep trying."
- Note: acknowledges the problem, suggests a fix, and reassures the user.

**Loading failures:**
- "Something went wrong. We're having trouble loading your messages. Try refreshing."
- Note: honest, brief, actionable.

**The key patterns:**
1. No error codes shown to users
2. No blame on the user
3. Always a suggested next step
4. Calm, friendly tone even when things break
5. Personality is muted during errors — not absent, but quieter

### Empty States

Slack excels at empty states that feel warm, not barren:

- **No messages:** "This is the very beginning of your conversation with [name]."
- **No search results:** "Nothing turned up for [query]. You could try a different search."
- **No channels:** "Channels are where your team communicates. Create one to get started."

Each empty state: explains what belongs here, why it's empty, and what to do next.

### Loading Messages

Slack's loading screen messages became iconic for injecting personality into a moment of waiting:
- "Herding cats..."
- "Adjusting flux capacitor..."
- "Reticulating splines..."

This works because: (a) loading is a low-stakes moment, (b) it reduces perceived wait time, (c) it reinforces brand personality without interfering with task completion.

### Lessons for Content Designers

1. **Personality has a volume dial.** Full volume in loading screens. Medium in onboarding. Low in errors. Off in legal text.
2. **Consistency builds trust.** Slack sounds like Slack on every screen.
3. **"We" vs. "I" vs. "Slack":** They use "we" to take responsibility for problems and "you/your" to give credit to users.
4. **Specificity over generics:** "Trouble connecting to Slack" is better than "An error occurred."
5. **Words replace UI when needed:** Their loading messages prove that text alone can create an experience.
