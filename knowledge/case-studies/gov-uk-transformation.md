### Overview

GOV.UK's content transformation is the most significant content design case study in history. They reduced a 75,000-page government web presence to ~3,000 pages while improving user satisfaction, task completion, and accessibility. It proved that content design is a discipline, not decoration.

### The Problem (Before 2012)

- 75,000+ pages across hundreds of government websites
- Written by policy experts for policy experts — not citizens
- Average reading level: Grade 15+ (postgraduate)
- Users couldn't find what they needed, didn't understand what they found
- Call centers overwhelmed with questions answered on the website (but incomprehensibly)

### The Transformation

**Strategy:** Every piece of content must answer a user need. If no one needs it, don't publish it.

**Process:**
1. Audit all content against analytics data and user research
2. Identify user needs through search queries, call center logs, and usability testing
3. Rewrite content in plain English targeting a 9-year-old reading level
4. Structure around tasks, not organizational charts
5. Test with real users, iterate, publish

**Key decisions:**
- Killed PDFs for essential information (not accessible, not searchable)
- One page per task, not one page per department
- Banned jargon — created an A-Z word replacement list
- Made content designers equal partners with developers and designers

### Results

- **Reduced pages from 75,000 to ~3,000** (96% reduction)
- **Task completion rates improved 30-50%** on redesigned services
- **Call center volume dropped significantly** for well-designed services
- **User satisfaction scores exceeded 80%** consistently
- **Global influence:** 30+ countries adopted GOV.UK's approach

### Content Design as a Discipline

GOV.UK created the role of "content designer" — someone who:
- Uses evidence and data to determine content needs
- Writes content that is clear, useful, and accessible
- Works alongside interaction designers and developers
- Iterates based on user feedback and analytics
- Advocates for users within the organization

This was revolutionary. Before GOV.UK, content was typically written by subject matter experts and reviewed by communications teams. GOV.UK proved that writing for the web requires specialized skills.

### The "Pair Writing" Method

GOV.UK pioneered pair writing:
1. A subject matter expert provides the knowledge
2. A content designer structures and simplifies the language
3. Together they produce content that is accurate AND accessible

This resolved the classic tension between technical accuracy and plain language.

### Lessons for Product Content Design

1. **Measure everything.** Analytics, search logs, and user testing should drive content decisions.
2. **Less is more — literally.** Cutting 96% of content improved the experience.
3. **Reading level targets work.** Targeting Grade 4 helps *everyone*, including PhD holders.
4. **Content design is a practice, not a polish step.** Involve content designers from the start.
5. **Users don't care about your org chart.** Structure content by task, not by team or department.
6. **Plain language is not "dumbing down."** It's doing the hard work to make complex things understandable.
