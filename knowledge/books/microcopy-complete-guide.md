### About the Book

Kinneret Yifrah's *Microcopy: The Complete Guide* is the most comprehensive resource specifically about the small words that power interfaces — button labels, error messages, tooltips, placeholder text, confirmation dialogs, and more. It's the tactical companion to more strategic content design books.

### Core Thesis

Microcopy — the small pieces of text in a user interface — has disproportionate impact on user experience, conversion rates, and trust. A single word change on a button can move conversion rates by 10-30%. Microcopy is where content design meets business results.

### Key Principles

**1. Every word is a UX decision.**
- "Submit" vs. "Create account" vs. "Get started" — each creates a different expectation
- Button labels set expectations for what happens next
- Error messages determine whether users recover or abandon

**2. Microcopy reduces friction.**
- Friction exists wherever users pause, hesitate, or get confused
- Good microcopy anticipates confusion and preempts it
- Example: "We'll never share your email" next to an email field reduces form abandonment

**3. Microcopy builds trust.**
- Transparency about what happens after an action ("You can change this later")
- Honesty about costs and commitments ("Free for 14 days, then $9/month")
- Reassurance at anxiety points ("Your payment is secure" with a lock icon)

### Microcopy by Component

**Buttons:**
- Use specific verbs: "Add to cart" > "Submit"
- Match the label to the user's mental model: "Place order" > "Process transaction"
- Primary actions: verb + noun ("Save changes")
- Destructive actions: be explicit ("Delete 5 files permanently")

**Forms:**
- Labels > placeholders (placeholders disappear, labels persist)
- Format hints below the field: "Must be at least 8 characters"
- Inline validation: "Looks good!" or "Passwords don't match"
- Smart defaults: pre-fill what you can, pre-select the most common option

**Error messages:**
The three-part formula:
1. What happened: "That email is already registered"
2. Why: "An account with this email already exists"
3. What to do: "Sign in instead, or use a different email"

**Empty states:**
The three jobs of an empty state:
1. Explain what belongs here
2. Show the user isn't lost
3. Prompt the first action

**Tooltips:**
- Answer ONE question per tooltip
- Front-load the key information
- Keep under 60 characters if possible
- Should enhance, never replace, the primary interface

**Confirmation dialogs:**
- Title: What's about to happen ("Delete this project?")
- Body: Consequences ("This removes all files and team access permanently")
- Primary button: Matches the title verb ("Delete project")
- Secondary button: Clear escape ("Keep project")

### Conversion-Focused Microcopy

**Reducing form abandonment:**
- Explain why you need information: "We use your phone number for delivery updates only"
- Show progress: "Step 2 of 3"
- Minimize fields: every field you remove increases completion rate
- Social proof near CTAs: "Join 50,000+ teams"

**Reducing checkout friction:**
- "Free shipping" and "Free returns" near the buy button
- "Cancel anytime" near subscription CTAs
- "Takes about 2 minutes" for sign-up flows
- Security indicators: "256-bit encryption" or lock icon near payment fields

### Actionable Takeaways

- **A/B test your microcopy.** Small word changes create measurable business impact.
- **Write microcopy in context.** Never write a button label in a spreadsheet — always in the actual screen.
- **Audit friction points.** Where do users drop off? The microcopy there is probably the problem (or the solution).
- **Build a microcopy library.** Standard patterns for buttons, errors, empty states, tooltips — consistency at scale.
- **Character count matters.** Set maximums per component and enforce them.
