### About the Book

Torrey Podmajersky's *Strategic Writing for UX* provides a systematic approach to UX writing that scales across teams and products. The standout contribution is the "voice chart" — a structured tool for making voice and tone decisions consistent and repeatable.

### Core Thesis

UX writing shouldn't be ad hoc. It needs strategy, systems, and structure — just like visual design has design systems. Individual brilliance doesn't scale; systematic approaches do.

### The Voice Chart

Podmajersky's most influential framework — a table that maps voice dimensions to specific writing decisions:

| Dimension | Product voice | Concepts | Vocabulary | Verbosity | Grammar | Punctuation |
|-----------|--------------|----------|-----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Example row | Confident, helpful | Explain what users can do | Common words, no jargon | Concise, direct | Active voice, present tense | Periods, no exclamation marks |

**How to use it:**
- Each column constrains a specific writing decision
- New writers can follow the chart to produce on-brand content immediately
- The chart makes voice discussions concrete, not abstract
- Update the chart as the brand evolves

### UX Text Patterns

Podmajersky identifies recurring text types and provides frameworks for each:

**Titles:**
- Tell users where they are
- Use nouns or noun phrases: "Account settings," "Payment method"
- Be specific: "Your orders" > "Orders" > "List"

**Descriptions:**
- Explain what something is or does
- Lead with the benefit or function
- One sentence when possible

**Actions (buttons/links):**
- Start with a verb: "Save," "Delete," "Create account"
- Be specific about outcome: "Place order" > "Continue"
- Use title case or sentence case consistently

**Empty states:**
- What belongs here + how to fill it
- "No notifications yet. You'll see updates here when your team makes changes."

**Confirmation messages:**
- What just happened + what comes next
- "Order placed. You'll receive a confirmation email in a few minutes."

**Error messages:**
- What went wrong + how to fix it
- "Card declined. Check your card number or try a different payment method."

### The Content-First Approach

1. **Start with the user's goal** — not the screen, not the feature
2. **Write the content first** — before wireframes or mockups
3. **Design around the content** — not the other way around
4. **Test the content** — in context, with real users
5. **Iterate** — content and design evolve together

### Measuring UX Writing

Podmajersky advocates for measurable content:
- **Task completion rate** — Can users accomplish their goal?
- **Time on task** — How long does it take?
- **Error rate** — How often do users make mistakes?
- **Support ticket volume** — Are users confused enough to ask for help?
- **Content satisfaction** — Do users find the content helpful?

### Actionable Takeaways

- **Build a voice chart** for your product. It makes voice concrete and teachable.
- **Create text patterns** for recurring components. Consistency at scale.
- **Measure content effectiveness** with the same rigor as visual design.
- **Content-first design** prevents "Where does this long error message fit?" problems.
- **Systems > individuals.** A good voice chart helps any writer produce on-brand content.
- **UX writing is a practice.** It needs principles, tools, and iteration — not just good instincts.
